Aetna Clinical Policy Bulletin: Lumbar Spinal Fusion Surgery
Policy Number: CPB 0743 | Effective Date: January 1, 2024

POLICY SUMMARY
Aetna considers lumbar spinal fusion surgery medically necessary for the treatment of
degenerative disc disease, spondylolisthesis, spinal stenosis, or disc herniation when
specific clinical criteria are met.

CLINICAL CRITERIA â€” ALL of the following must be documented:

1. IMAGING CONFIRMATION
   MRI or CT confirms disc herniation, spinal stenosis, or spondylolisthesis with
   clinical symptoms that correlate with the structural findings. Dynamic instability
   may require flexion/extension radiographs.

2. PHYSICAL THERAPY REQUIREMENT
   Patient has completed >= 12 weeks of structured physical therapy with documented
   functional outcome scores (ODI, VAS, or equivalent). Therapy must include core
   stabilization, flexibility training, and progressive strengthening.

3. FAILED INTERVENTIONAL PROCEDURES
   At least 2 failed epidural steroid injections (ESI) or selective nerve root blocks
   performed under fluoroscopic guidance, with documented response to each injection.

4. PHARMACOTHERAPY FAILURE
   Documented failure of multimodal pharmacotherapy including:
   - NSAIDs (minimum 4-week trial)
   - Muscle relaxants
   - Neuropathic agents (gabapentin, pregabalin, or duloxetine)

5. FUNCTIONAL DISABILITY
   Oswestry Disability Index (ODI) >= 40% or Visual Analog Scale (VAS) pain score
   >= 7/10, measured within 30 days of the authorization request.

6. BMI REQUIREMENT
   BMI < 35, or documented enrollment in a medically-supervised weight management
   program if BMI >= 35.

7. TOBACCO CESSATION
   No active tobacco use, or documented completion of a tobacco cessation program.

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION FOR SUBMISSION:
- MRI or CT report (within 12 months)
- Physical therapy records with functional outcome scores
- ESI/nerve block procedure notes with response documentation
- Current medication list with trial durations and outcomes
- ODI/VAS scores (within 30 days)
- BMI documentation
- Tobacco use screening
