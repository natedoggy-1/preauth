UnitedHealthcare Medical Policy: Lumbar Spinal Fusion
Policy Number: 2023T0547 | Effective Date: January 1, 2024

POLICY SUMMARY
UnitedHealthcare considers lumbar spinal fusion medically necessary for patients with
documented structural spinal pathology who have failed adequate conservative management
and meet specific clinical and psychosocial criteria.

CLINICAL CRITERIA â€” ALL of the following are required:

1. ADVANCED IMAGING
   MRI (preferred) or CT demonstrating structural pathology including but not limited
   to disc herniation, spinal stenosis, spondylolisthesis, or segmental instability.
   Findings must correlate with clinical presentation.

2. NON-OPERATIVE TREATMENT
   At least 3 months (12 weeks) of non-operative treatment including supervised
   physical therapy with documented attendance and functional progress measures.

3. NEUROLOGICAL OR FUNCTIONAL CRITERIA
   Progressive neurological deficit (motor weakness, bowel/bladder dysfunction), OR
   functionally disabling symptoms that impair activities of daily living despite
   conservative management.

4. INTERVENTIONAL PROCEDURE FAILURE
   Failure of at least one interventional pain procedure:
   - Epidural steroid injection (ESI)
   - Selective nerve root block
   - Facet joint injection
   Documented response required for each procedure.

5. MEDICAL OPTIMIZATION
   Patient is medically optimized for surgery:
   - HbA1c < 8.0% if diabetic (within 90 days)
   - Nutritional status adequate
   - Comorbidities managed

6. PSYCHOSOCIAL SCREENING
   Psychosocial screening completed using a validated instrument (PHQ-9 or equivalent).
   No untreated psychiatric conditions that would impair surgical outcome.

7. SURGICAL PLAN LIMITS
   Surgical plan includes no more than 3 motion segments (3 interbody levels).
   Plans exceeding 3 levels require additional peer-to-peer review.

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION:
- MRI or CT report (within 12 months)
- Physical therapy records with functional outcome measures
- Interventional procedure notes with response data
- HbA1c lab results (if diabetic)
- PHQ-9 or psychosocial screening results
- Surgical plan with proposed levels
