Humana Medical Coverage Policy: Epidural Steroid Injection (ESI)
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

POLICY SUMMARY
Humana considers epidural steroid injection medically necessary for patients with
documented radiculopathy or spinal stenosis who have failed adequate conservative
management and have imaging correlation.

CLINICAL CRITERIA â€” ALL of the following must be met:

1. DIAGNOSIS WITH IMAGING CORRELATION
   Documented radiculopathy or spinal stenosis confirmed by:
   - Clinical examination findings (dermatomal pain, neurological deficits)
   - MRI or CT demonstrating structural pathology correlating with symptoms

2. CONSERVATIVE MANAGEMENT
   At least 6 weeks of conservative management including:
   - Physical therapy (structured program)
   - Oral analgesics (NSAIDs, neuropathic agents, or muscle relaxants)

3. INJECTION FREQUENCY LIMITS
   Maximum 3 injections per spinal region per rolling 12-month period.
   Spinal regions counted separately: cervical, thoracic, lumbar.

4. IMAGE GUIDANCE REQUIREMENT
   Fluoroscopic or CT guidance required for all spinal injection procedures.
   Blind (non-image-guided) injections are not covered.

5. REPEAT INJECTION CRITERIA
   For repeat injections in the same region:
   - Prior injection must have provided >= 50% pain relief
   - Relief must have lasted >= 2 weeks
   - Documented functional improvement from prior injection

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION:
- MRI or CT report correlating with symptoms
- Physical therapy records
- Medication history with trial outcomes
- Prior injection records (if repeat injection)

APPLICABLE CPT CODES:
62321, 62322, 62323, 64483, 64484

APPEAL DEADLINE: 45 calendar days from denial notification
